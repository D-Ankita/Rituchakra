# Rajasvala Charya for a Working Woman

The classical Ayurvedic regimen was written for a world that is not the world you live in. Rest for three days. Sleep on a simple bed. Quiet company. No strenuous activity.

You have a meeting at 10. Your commute is 90 minutes. Your 4-year-old wakes up at 6:30. Your team has a deadline Friday. You do not have the luxury of opting out of your life for three days every month.

So what does *rajasvala-charya* look like when you can't stop? The classical texts wouldn't want you to feel guilty about the gap. They'd want you to take what you can from the principle.

Here is a practical guide.

## The three commitments (that actually fit)

Forget the rest. Forget the full regimen. If you keep *three things* during days 1-2 of your period, you will have done 80% of what the classical texts ask.

### 1. Warm food, twice a day

Most important thing. If everything else falls apart, keep this.

- **Breakfast:** oatmeal with cinnamon, or upma, or leftover dal-rice warmed up. Skip cold cereal, smoothie, fruit alone.
- **Lunch:** warm, cooked. Dal-rice. Khichdi. Roti-sabji. Not a salad.
- **Hydration:** warm water throughout the day. Jeera (cumin) water if you can. Skip iced drinks.

If you can pre-make a pot of kitchari on day 0 (the day before your period usually starts), you're set for 2 days. Reheats beautifully.

### 2. One hour earlier to bed

Not three. One.

If you normally sleep at 11, aim for 10 during days 1-2. Not perfect — just earlier. Sleep is where the body does most of its vata-balancing work.

- **Screen off by 9:30** on day 1 night if possible.
- **Warm milk with a pinch of cardamom + turmeric** 30 min before sleep — classical *nidra-jananam* (sleep-inducing) preparation.
- **Heat pack or hot water bottle on the belly** while winding down.

### 3. Scale back the "extra"

You can't skip the mandatory. But you can skip the extra:

- **Workouts:** gentle yoga or walk only. Not HIIT, not heavy lifting, not a long run. Restart in follicular.
- **Social plans:** RSVP quietly no to the optional wedding sangeet on day 2. Go to the mandatory family gathering if you must, but leave early.
- **Work stretch:** do the core work. Skip the volunteer-y extras. That committee meeting you could skip — skip it.

## Managing the work-day itself

**In the morning:**
- Hot water before anything else
- Warm breakfast (not smoothie, not cold yoghurt)
- A slightly softer outfit than usual — no tight jeans on day 1 if you can avoid
- Apply warm sesame oil on lower belly + back before shower (5 min ritual, genuinely helps cramps)
- Leave the house 10 min earlier if it means avoiding the stressed rush

**During the day:**
- **Keep a thermos of warm water at your desk.** Drink from it, not from the cold water dispenser.
- **Take the cramping seriously.** If you have them, take ibuprofen or your usual pain reliever. "Powering through" is not virtuous; it worsens vata.
- **Eat your lunch sitting down.** Actually eat it. Don't inhale it between Zoom calls.
- **Afternoon:** avoid cold drinks, cold food. Warm tea instead.
- **Save difficult conversations for follicular.** The big confrontation, the hard feedback session — push them off 3-4 days if you can.

**After work:**
- Skip the usual gym. Walk if you have energy. Or nothing.
- Warm shower, not cold.
- Early, warm dinner. Kitchari, soup, dal-rice.
- Wind down slowly.

## The apology you don't owe

You do not have to apologize for:
- Saying no to social events on day 1-2
- Turning down optional work
- Needing to rest
- Needing warmth

These are the modern expressions of an ancient regimen. Your grandmother knew it — she just called it something else.

## What to do if pain is severe

Rajasvala-charya is not a substitute for medical care. If you have:
- Pain that interferes with your ability to function
- Pain that doesn't respond to ibuprofen
- Pain that has worsened over months or years
- Heavy flow that causes fatigue or anemia

— see a gynecologist. Endometriosis, adenomyosis, fibroids, and uterine conditions are underdiagnosed. A warm meal and a heating pack are not the answer to pathology.

## The week after

Classical Ayurveda doesn't stop at menstruation. *Rutu-kala* (follicular phase) has its own supports — nourishing food, rebuilding rituals, more abhyanga — designed to replenish what menses released.

So days 5-10 are your building week. Eat more, cook more, rest well, lift weights, sleep long. By day 10, you'll feel the difference.

This is not ancient mysticism. It's cyclical self-care. The body is cyclical. The care is cyclical. The benefits compound across months.
