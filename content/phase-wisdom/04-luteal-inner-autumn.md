# Luteal · The Inner Autumn

Around day 18, something shifts. The people you could see all week now feel like too many people. The clarity that came easily in ovulation gets less sharp. The body wants softer food, warmer showers, longer sleep. You might notice breast tenderness, a touch of bloat, a thread of melancholy you can't quite place.

This is not PMS. This is your inner autumn. It happens every single cycle, and it is not a bug — it is a feature.

## The body

The dominant follicle that released an egg during ovulation has become the *corpus luteum* — literally "yellow body" — a temporary endocrine gland. Its job, for the next twelve to fourteen days, is to produce **progesterone**.

Progesterone is the ruling hormone of the luteal phase. It does several distinct things:

- **Maintains the endometrial lining.** If pregnancy occurs, progesterone's job is to hold the uterine environment welcoming until the placenta takes over. If pregnancy does not occur, progesterone levels fall at the end of luteal — and that fall triggers menstruation.
- **Raises basal body temperature** by about 0.3–0.5°C. The body is measurably warmer in luteal than in follicular.
- **Increases metabolic rate by ~10%**. You are burning more calories at rest. You are also hungrier. This is normal. Do not suppress it.
- **Influences GABA receptors** in the brain — mildly sedating, mildly anxiolytic (in early luteal). But progesterone's metabolite *allopregnanolone* can have paradoxical effects in late luteal as it withdraws, contributing to anxiety, tearfulness, and irritability. This is the hormonal basis of PMS in many people.
- **Affects gut motility, fluid retention, breast tissue, and skin** — hence the bloating, tenderness, and premenstrual acne many people notice.

Late luteal — the last 3–5 days before menses — is when progesterone plummets. *Estrogen* also drops. Serotonin follows. This rapid hormonal withdrawal is not emotional weakness. It is chemistry.

## What the classical texts say

Ayurveda describes the luteal phase as the *Pitta-to-Vata transition*. Early luteal — roughly day 15 through 22 — remains Pitta-dominated. The body is still warm (BBT is elevated), the appetite is stronger, agni is active. Metabolism is at its peak of the cycle. This is why late afternoon snacking often peaks in early luteal — the body legitimately wants more fuel.

Late luteal — day 23 through 28 — shifts toward *Vata*. The *Sushruta Samhita* notes that as the body prepares once again for *rajah-srava*, Vata (specifically *apana vayu*) begins to take its place. This is felt as dryness, cold sensitivity (paradoxically alongside the warmth of elevated BBT), scattered attention, heightened emotional sensitivity, and sometimes insomnia or vivid dreams. The body is *turning*.

Classical Ayurveda does not have a single term exactly corresponding to "PMS." Instead, it treats the cluster of pre-menstrual symptoms as signs that Vata is arriving imperfectly — that *apana* is not yet moving cleanly downward, and the body has not yet released what it needs to release. The prescription, across all major texts, is consistent: warmth, grounding, rest, moist-unctuous food, routine, and the avoidance of *vata-aggravating* activities (travel, intense exertion, cold food, emotional turbulence, skipped meals, fragmented sleep).

Maya Tiwari describes this phase as the *inward turn*: the time when the body gathers in what it harvested from ovulation's outward expression and begins the quiet work of preparing to release what is no longer needed. It is, in her framing, a deeply private time.

## What this means for today

The luteal phase rewards softening. Here is what that looks like in practice:

1. **Eat warm, cooked, grounding food.** Kitchari. Dal and rice. Root vegetables. Ghee. This is *not* the week for raw salads, smoothies, cold water, or restrictive eating. Your body wants fuel.
2. **Sleep longer, and go to bed earlier.** The melatonin rhythm shifts in luteal. Honor it. 9:30–10 pm bedtimes often work better this week than 11 pm.
3. **Less exertion, more steadiness.** Trade HIIT for long walks, yin yoga, gentle swimming, strength training at 70%. This is not a week for PRs.
4. **Plan less, execute more.** The forward-momentum of follicular and ovulation — use those for planning. Luteal is for finishing what you started, for detail work, for editing, for closing loops. Your brain is actually good at detail work in luteal.
5. **Warm oil grounds Vata.** Abhyanga with warm sesame oil, especially in late luteal, soothes the descending Vata and often reduces PMS symptoms significantly. Pay attention to the feet, lower back, and belly.
6. **Cook with warming spices.** Ginger, cumin, cardamom, cinnamon, clove. Avoid too much raw garlic, chili, or vinegar in late luteal — these can heat pitta while vata is rising, a combination that tends to intensify mood symptoms.
7. **Make space for emotion.** Tears you can't explain, a sudden wave of grief or anger or softness — luteal brings unprocessed material up to the surface. This is not dysfunction. This is the body clearing. Let it.
8. **Reduce social input.** You are not cancelled. You are saving your energy for what matters. It is perfectly reasonable to say, "I'm in luteal, I'd love to see you next week."
9. **Magnesium, B6, and omega-3** are the three nutrients most supported by research for luteal comfort. Pumpkin seeds, dark leafy greens, walnuts, flaxseeds. Ask your doctor before supplementing.

If only one habit: warm food + early sleep. That alone often changes how you experience PMS.

## When to see a doctor

Please consult a physician if you experience:

- **PMDD symptoms** — severe mood symptoms (rage, deep depression, suicidal thinking) that cluster in late luteal and resolve by day 3 of bleeding. This is a real condition with real treatment.
- Luteal phase consistently shorter than 10 days — can indicate a luteal phase defect and is relevant for fertility.
- Spotting throughout luteal.
- Severe breast pain or lumps that persist beyond one cycle.
- Migraines that cluster in late luteal and don't respond to usual management.
- Severe insomnia or anxiety that significantly disrupts function.
- Suspected pregnancy — missed period is just one sign; take a test if there's any possibility.

Luteal is also when endometriosis and adenomyosis often "speak loudest" — if your pre-menstrual pain is disabling or worsening over years, do not accept "it's just PMS." Seek evaluation.
