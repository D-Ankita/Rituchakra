# Progesterone · The Hormone That Holds

If estrogen is the *building* hormone of your cycle, progesterone is the *holding* hormone. It's produced for roughly 14 days — from ovulation until the start of your next period. It is the ruling hormone of your luteal phase, and understanding it explains most of what happens in the two weeks before your period.

## What it is

A steroid hormone produced primarily by the **corpus luteum** — the "yellow body" that forms from a ruptured ovarian follicle after it releases its egg. The corpus luteum's entire purpose for 14 days is to produce progesterone (and some estrogen).

If pregnancy occurs, the corpus luteum continues until the placenta takes over progesterone production around week 10.
If not, the corpus luteum degenerates, progesterone crashes, and that crash triggers menstruation.

The peak around day 21 is why a "day 21 progesterone test" is the standard confirmation that ovulation occurred.

## What it does — biologically

**Uterine:**
- Stabilizes the endometrial lining — holds it in place so pregnancy *could* implant
- When progesterone crashes (day 27-28), lining sheds → menstruation begins

**Body temperature:**
- Raises basal body temperature by **0.3-0.5°C**
- This is the basis of fertility awareness methods — BBT rising confirms ovulation happened

**Metabolism:**
- Raises basal metabolic rate by **~10%** — you burn more calories at rest
- Increases appetite (especially for carbs) — this is biology, not lack of willpower
- Slightly raises blood sugar (progesterone creates a mild insulin resistance state)

**Brain:**
- **Allopregnanolone** (progesterone's main metabolite) acts on GABA-A receptors — the same receptors affected by alcohol and anti-anxiety medications
- In early-to-mid luteal: mildly sedating, mildly anxiolytic → many women feel *calm* in mid-luteal
- In late luteal: the rapid *withdrawal* of allopregnanolone is a leading hypothesis for PMS mood symptoms

**Other:**
- Mild diuretic effect initially, then fluid retention toward end
- Breast tissue responds: tenderness, slight enlargement (glandular support for possible pregnancy)
- Gut motility slows (mild constipation in luteal is normal)
- Cervical mucus turns thick and scant (classical marker of post-ovulatory infertile state)

## What Ayurveda says about the same phase

Classical Ayurveda recognized the same transition:
- **Early luteal (day 15-22)** = *pitta-dominated* → warm, active, metabolic
- **Late luteal (day 23-28)** = *vata-arriving* → the body preparing once again for *rajah-srava*

The body is *literally warmer* in luteal (measurable on BBT) — Ayurveda's "pitta persists" description is not metaphor.
The late-luteal mood drop, bloat, and scattered feeling — Ayurveda identifies these as *vata aggravation* as apana begins to assert its downward flow.

## Why PMS happens (the hormonal mechanism)

The leading scientific hypothesis for PMS and PMDD:
1. In late luteal, progesterone falls rapidly (days 24-28)
2. Allopregnanolone (its GABA-modulating metabolite) falls with it
3. GABAergic tone in the brain drops → anxiety rises, sleep worsens, irritability emerges
4. In people whose brains are more sensitive to allopregnanolone shifts, PMDD emerges — not a mild version of PMS, but a significantly disabling mood disorder

This is why:
- Some SSRIs work for PMDD (they modulate the GABA-serotonin system)
- Birth control pills can help or worsen PMS depending on the individual
- The symptoms resolve within 2-3 days of bleeding (progesterone stabilizes low, GABA system re-equilibrates)

PMS is not imagined. It's not weakness. It's neurochemistry.

## What goes wrong, and when to check

Low progesterone in luteal phase (should be peaking at day 21) can signal:
- **Anovulation** — no ovulation, no corpus luteum, no progesterone
- **Luteal phase defect** — corpus luteum functioning poorly
- **Stress** — cortisol competes with progesterone precursors
- **Thyroid imbalance**

Signs worth a day-21 progesterone test:
- Luteal phase consistently shorter than 10 days
- Very short cycles
- Spotting in luteal
- Difficulty conceiving
- Severe PMS that doesn't respond to usual care

## What supports healthy progesterone

- **Stable blood sugar** — insulin and progesterone compete for attention from the pregnenolone pool
- **Managed stress** — high cortisol steals progesterone precursors (the "pregnenolone steal")
- **Sleep** — especially in late luteal
- **Zinc + B6 + magnesium** — cofactors for hormone synthesis
- **Adequate fat intake** — cholesterol is the precursor to all steroid hormones
- **Not over-exercising in luteal** — intensity interferes with the HPA axis

## What doesn't

- "Natural progesterone" creams without practitioner oversight
- Over-the-counter progesterone supplements (in India, these are prescription-only)
- Excessive fasting or caloric restriction in luteal (will delay or worsen menses)
