# Your First Ritual · A Breathing-In

A ritual is not a big thing. It's a small agreement with yourself — the same one, at the same time, for the same two minutes — that over weeks becomes the spine of a different relationship with your body.

This is where we start. Two minutes. Any day. Any phase.

## The ritual

1. **Sit comfortably.** Chair, floor, bed. Cross-legged, knees-together, whatever doesn't hurt. If sitting is too much, lie down — that's fine too.
2. **Place one hand on your belly**, low, below the navel. This is where *apana vayu* lives in Ayurveda. The hand is a small, warm presence. Notice its weight.
3. **Close your eyes or soften your gaze downward.**
4. **Take one slow breath** — in through the nose, letting the belly rise into your hand. Out through the nose, letting the belly fall. That's one. Let it be slower than normal, but not forced.
5. **Take nine more.** Ten breaths total. That's it.
6. **Before you open your eyes**, ask silently: *what part of me needs attention today?* Don't answer with words. Just listen for a second. Then open your eyes.

## Why this works

Ayurveda begins every prescribed daily routine — *dinacharya* — with breath work. The reasoning is practical: breath is what connects the nervous system to the body's unconscious rhythms. Slow the breath, and you slow the sympathetic-nervous-system churn that runs most of us most of the day. *Apana vayu*, the downward current that governs your menstrual cycle, is particularly sensitive to breath. Two minutes of slow belly-breathing is a literal invitation for the cycle's downward flow to be unobstructed.

Modern research agrees. Slow nasal breathing (~6 breaths per minute) increases vagal tone, decreases cortisol, and improves heart rate variability within a single session. Ten breaths is a gentle dose but a real one.

## When to do it

- **Morning:** before checking your phone. This one change — breath before screen — quietly changes your whole day.
- **Afternoon:** after lunch, before opening the next task. A small reset.
- **Night:** before sleep. One of the most effective insomnia interventions studied.

Any one of these. Don't try all three yet.

## What comes next

After a week of this, you'll notice small things: you reach for the phone slower. The transition between tasks gets softer. You notice your own belly rising and falling during random moments of the day — a kind of gentle self-awareness that wasn't there before.

That's the whole point. You're not fixing anything. You're paying attention.
