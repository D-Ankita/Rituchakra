# Triphala · The Three Fruits

If you asked a vaidya to name the single herbal formula they'd prescribe most often, the answer is usually triphala. It is the **quiet workhorse** of Ayurvedic pharmacy — gentle enough for daily use, steady enough that the benefits build over months.

*Triphala* literally means "three fruits." Three dried fruits, ground to powder, combined in specific proportions:
- **Haritaki** (*Terminalia chebula*) — pacifies vata
- **Bibhitaki** (*Terminalia bellirica*) — pacifies kapha
- **Amalaki** (*Phyllanthus emblica* / Indian gooseberry) — pacifies pitta

The combination is *tridoshic* — balancing for all three doshas, and therefore usable by nearly everyone.

## Why it matters for your cycle

Classical Ayurveda connects digestive health (*agni* — digestive fire) with menstrual health. When *agni* is weak, the whole cycle tends to go off:
- Scanty or irregular cycles
- Bloating, gas, constipation around menses
- Brain fog and fatigue, especially in luteal
- Skin congestion, acne, dullness

**Triphala supports agni.** It gently cleanses the digestive tract, supports regular bowel movement (essential for healthy apana vayu — the downward flow that governs menstruation), and provides deep antioxidant support for all tissues.

More specifically:
- **Haritaki** acts as a mild, non-habit-forming laxative. Useful when apana is blocked.
- **Amalaki** is one of the richest natural sources of vitamin C and bioflavonoids. Cooling for pitta, supports the skin, hair, eyes, and immunity.
- **Bibhitaki** helps clear kapha (mucus, stagnation) from the respiratory and digestive channels.

The three work together in a way no single one does alone.

## What modern research supports

Triphala has more published research than most classical formulas:

- **Antioxidant activity** — one of the most potent plant-based antioxidants documented
- **Anti-inflammatory** — across multiple pathways
- **Gentle laxative** — without the dependency risk of stimulant laxatives (senna, cascara)
- **Gut microbiome support** — emerging research on prebiotic effects
- **Oral health** — antimicrobial, used in mouth rinses for gingivitis
- **Metabolic support** — modest effects on cholesterol and blood sugar in small trials
- **Weight support** — very modest effects, appropriately so

What it is NOT:
- A magic cure for anything
- A substitute for medical treatment
- Something that works in 2 days (it works in 2 months)

## When triphala may help (in the context of cycle health)

- **Constipation, especially before or during menses** — apana flow is essential; triphala gently supports it
- **Irregular cycles associated with digestive imbalance** — treating agni first is often upstream of treating cycle symptoms
- **Sluggish mornings, heavy kapha** — triphala's mild cleansing action lifts morning heaviness
- **Daily antioxidant support** — particularly in modern urban living (pollution, stress, processed food)
- **Post-menstrual recovery** — supporting the rebuilding of rasa and rakta dhatu

## When triphala may NOT be right for you

- **Active loose stools / diarrhea** — the laxative action will worsen it
- **Severe anorexia / underweight** — the cleansing action is not what's needed
- **Pregnancy** — classical texts and modern caution agree: avoid during pregnancy
- **Dehydration** — balance with good water intake
- **Acute illness with digestive weakness** — wait until recovery

## How it's traditionally taken

- **Powder (churna):** 3-5 g (about 1 teaspoon) before bed, mixed with warm water, honey (if not hot-weather season), or almond milk
- **Tablet form:** 500-1000 mg before bed — easier for those who don't like the strong astringent taste
- **Liquid decoction:** stronger, more traditional, less convenient
- **Starting dose:** start with half the suggested amount for the first week, assess response

**Timing:** traditionally taken at night, away from food, for best effect on the next morning's elimination. Some traditions recommend morning use — both work.

**Duration:** safe for long-term use (6+ months). Some vaidyas recommend breaks (2 months on, 1 month off) to maintain sensitivity.

## Common mistakes

- **Treating it like a one-time cleanse** — triphala works via gentle daily action, not occasional use
- **Taking too much too fast** — start low, go slow; loose stools means pull back
- **Mixing with hot milk** — the tannins curdle it; use warm water or almond milk instead
- **Expecting results in a week** — realistic timeline is 6-8 weeks to notice a shift

## When to see a practitioner

- Chronic constipation lasting months
- Severe or chronic digestive issues (IBS-like patterns)
- Digestive weakness AND menstrual irregularity together — a vaidya will assess the whole picture
