# Lodhra · The Astringent Bark for the Uterus

Lodhra, *Symplocos racemosa*, is an evergreen tree native to South and Southeast Asia. Its bark is among the most frequently named herbs in classical Ayurvedic gynecology — often appearing alongside ashoka, *bala*, and *ashwagandha* in traditional uterine formulas.

The taste on the tongue is sharply astringent. The action it brings is binding — which is exactly what's wanted in some contexts and exactly what you want to avoid in others.

## What Ayurveda says it does

Lodhra's dominant qualities:
- **Sheeta (cooling)** — calms pitta
- **Kashaya (astringent)** — tightens, binds, holds
- **Grahi (absorbent)** — absorbs excess fluid
- **Raktastambhaka (hemostatic)** — helps stop bleeding

Classical indications from *Sushruta Samhita* and *Ashtanga Hridaya*:
- **Rakta-pradara** (heavy menstrual bleeding of pitta origin)
- **Shweta-pradara** (leucorrhea — excessive vaginal discharge)
- **Atisara** (loose stools — the same binding action works here)
- As part of skin formulations for pitta-related conditions
- Post-partum uterine support

Common classical preparations:
- **Lodhrasava** — fermented tonic
- **Lodhradi churna** — powder blend with other astringents
- **Stri-rasayana formulas** — paired with shatavari, ashoka, bala

## What modern research supports

The science on lodhra is limited but converging:
- **Active compounds** — alkaloids (loturine, colloturine), tannins, flavonoids
- **Hemostatic effect** — animal studies show reduced bleeding time
- **Anti-inflammatory** — significant in pre-clinical studies
- **Antimicrobial** — useful context for leucorrhea indications
- **Mild uterotonic** — some modulation of uterine muscle tone

We don't have large randomized human trials yet. Quality of available clinical studies is modest. Treat the research as *supportive* of traditional use, not confirmatory.

## When lodhra may help

- **Heavy menstrual bleeding** with a pitta pattern (bright red, warm, with inflammatory feel) — typically paired with ashoka
- **Leucorrhea** — especially with burning, discharge, and heat
- **Post-partum recovery** under practitioner supervision

## When lodhra may NOT be right

- **Scanty, dark, late periods (vata pattern)** — the binding action can make this worse
- **Constipation** — astringent + binding can compound it
- **Chronic dryness** — will aggravate
- **Pregnancy** — traditionally avoided
- **Taking iron supplements** — tannins bind iron; separate by 2+ hours

## How it's used traditionally

- **Lodhra churna:** 3-6 g twice daily with warm water or honey, after meals
- **Lodhrasava:** 15-30 ml twice daily with equal water
- **In formulated products:** found in Ashokarishta-adjacent preparations, Chandraprabha vati, and others

**Do not self-prescribe.** The appropriate dose, timing, and companion herbs depend on the specific pattern. Heavy bleeding is a symptom that always deserves a diagnosis first. An herb cannot replace that diagnostic work.

## When to see a doctor, not just take an herb

- Heavy bleeding lasting 7+ days
- Soaking through a pad every hour for 2+ hours
- Bleeding between cycles
- Suspected fibroids, polyps, or any structural cause
- Anemia symptoms
- Any sudden change in your pattern
